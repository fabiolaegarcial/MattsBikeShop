SILVER COMET TRAIL

4342 Floyd Road, Mableton, GA 30126

2

The Silver Comet Trail runs west through Cobb, Paulding, and Polk counties. This quiet, non-motorized, paved trail is for walkers, hikers, bicyclists, rollerbladers, horses, dog walkers, and is wheelchair accessible. The Silver Comet Trail is 61.5 miles long, and starts at the Mavell Road Trailhead in Smyrna, Georgia. It ends at the Georgia/Alabama state line, near Cedartown and The Esom Hill Trailhead.

2 COMMENTS

Jabari Clarke

Definitely a hidden gem. Great for a social distance meetup. Trail is about 60 miles end to end and could be considered easy to moderate. No major climbing and a lot of canopy cover. Small children had a blast and did not get tired. At one end is an open plaza / meditation area. Ideal for a book club or just some quiet time. Parking is a bit tricky. Apartment complex across the street has visitor parking but I wouldn't chance it. Try closer to Briar Vista elementary but not in its parking lot. Overall, great experience.

Cara O’Grady

Love this trail so much - very short and easy, but surprisingly peaceful for its location. We’ve been walking it since before Oldcastle worked on it and were/remain astonished by how much work and love the Oldcastle volunteers put into this little trail. Wonderful any time of year, but particularly beautiful in the spring and fall (although the tree canopy makes it one of the coolest outdoor spaces around in the summer!)
